3D MODELING

I was first introduced to 3D Modeling through a summer program at Penn, which ultimately inspired me to pursue computer graphics through the DMD Program. I've since taken on more large scale projects using Autodesk Maya and Arnold and I hope to continue working with 3D modeling in the future!

Apartment Model

    After dedicating a whole Pinterest board to inspiration for my campus apartment, I decided to create this 3D model of what I envision my future apartment to be. I've added various small details throughout the room that reflect my personality, such as my love for my friends, Taylor Swift, and Percy Jackson.

    Stuffed Cat

      A model of the squishiest stuffed cat I've ever held. I put extra attention into the seams and making the model reflect the softness of the plush. Bonus: You can also see him make an appearance in my apartment model!

      Female Character Model

        I was able to revisit character modeling in one of my classes, so I made a model of the main character of a children's book I had written and illustrated for my English class. This model is more low-poly in comparison to my first character model and it was interesting to see how different the process was with the new techniques.

        Oscar Character Model

          I made this character during a summer course at Penn (ESAP) and it was my first time using Maya. The character, Oscar, has a warrior backstory. He wears armadillo-influenced scaled armor and uses a flail as a weapon, which resembles a prehistoric armadillo tail.
